# Arduino Component Glossary

A quick reference guide for all the hardware components used in the Master Hub.


## 1. Resistor
Limits the flow of electrical current to protect delicate components.
[Explore Resistor in detail](#)


## 2. Capacitor
Stores and releases electrical energy, acting like a tiny battery.
[Explore Capacitor in detail](#)


## 3. Diode
Allows electricity to flow in only one direction, acting like a one-way valve.
[Explore Diode in detail](#)


## 4. Light Emitting Diode (LED)
A special type of diode that emits light when current passes through it.
[Explore LED in detail](#)


## 5. Transistor
Acts as a digital switch or amplifier for electrical signals.
[Explore Transistor in detail](#)


## 6. Breadboard
A plug-and-play board for building temporary circuits without soldering.
[Explore Breadboard in detail](#)


## 7. Jumper Wires
Used to connect different components together on a breadboard.
[Explore Jumper Wires in detail](#)


## 8. Arduino Uno
The microcontroller board that acts as the brain for your projects.
[Explore Arduino Uno in detail](#)


## 9. Microcontroller
A compact integrated circuit designed to govern a specific operation.
[Explore Microcontroller in detail](#)


## 10. Integrated Circuit (IC)
A tiny chip containing thousands of microscopic transistors.
[Explore Integrated Circuit in detail](#)


## 11. Potentiometer
A variable resistor that you can turn like a volume knob.
[Explore Potentiometer in detail](#)


## 12. Photoresistor (LDR)
A sensor that changes its resistance based on the amount of light.
[Explore Photoresistor in detail](#)


## 13. Thermistor
A temperature sensor whose resistance changes with heat.
[Explore Thermistor in detail](#)


## 14. Piezo Buzzer
A small speaker that generates sound when voltage is applied.
[Explore Piezo Buzzer in detail](#)


## 15. Push Button
A simple mechanical switch that closes a circuit when pressed.
[Explore Push Button in detail](#)


## 16. Relay
An electromechanical switch used to control high-voltage devices.
[Explore Relay in detail](#)


## 17. Servo Motor
A motor that can rotate to precise angles, usually between 0 and 180 degrees.
[Explore Servo Motor in detail](#)


## 18. Stepper Motor
A motor that rotates in discrete, precise steps.
[Explore Stepper Motor in detail](#)


## 19. DC Motor
A standard electric motor that spins continuously when powered.
[Explore DC Motor in detail](#)


## 20. Ultrasonic Sensor (HC-SR04)
Measures distance by bouncing sound waves off objects.
[Explore Ultrasonic Sensor in detail](#)


## 21. PIR Motion Sensor
Detects movement by sensing infrared heat emitted by bodies.
[Explore PIR Motion Sensor in detail](#)


## 22. Humidity Sensor (DHT11)
Measures the amount of water vapor in the air.
[Explore Humidity Sensor in detail](#)


## 23. Tilt Sensor
Detects orientation or inclination using a tiny metal ball inside.
[Explore Tilt Sensor in detail](#)


## 24. Flame Sensor
Detects the specific infrared light wavelengths emitted by fire.
[Explore Flame Sensor in detail](#)


## 25. Real-Time Clock (RTC)
Keeps accurate track of the date and time using a coin-cell battery, even if the main Arduino loses power.
[Explore Real-Time Clock in detail](#)


## 26. LCD Display (16x2)
A screen capable of displaying 2 rows of 16 text characters.
[Explore LCD Display in detail](#)


## 27. 7-Segment Display
An array of 7 LEDs arranged to display numbers 0-9.
[Explore 7-Segment Display in detail](#)


## 28. Membrane Keypad
A flat array of buttons used for number and code entry.
[Explore Membrane Keypad in detail](#)


## 29. RFID Reader
Reads wireless smart cards and key fobs using radio frequencies.
[Explore RFID Reader in detail](#)


## 30. IR Receiver
Decodes infrared flashes from devices like a TV remote.
[Explore IR Receiver in detail](#)


## 31. Shift Register
Expands the number of digital output pins on your Arduino.
[Explore Shift Register in detail](#)


## 32. Voltage Regulator
Maintains a constant output voltage regardless of input changes.
[Explore Voltage Regulator in detail](#)


## 33. ESP8266 Module
Adds WiFi capabilities to your Arduino projects.
[Explore ESP8266 Module in detail](#)


## 34. Bluetooth Module (HC-05)
Allows wireless serial communication with phones and computers.
[Explore Bluetooth Module in detail](#)


## 35. Soil Moisture Sensor
Measures the conductivity of dirt to detect if plants need water.
[Explore Soil Moisture Sensor in detail](#)


## 36. Water Level Sensor
Detects depth by measuring resistance across parallel conductive traces.
[Explore Water Level Sensor in detail](#)


## 37. Laser Diode
Emits a highly focused, concentrated beam of light.
[Explore Laser Diode in detail](#)


## 38. Logic Level Shifter
Safely converts signals between devices operating at different voltages (typically 5V and 3.3V).
[Explore Logic Level Shifter in detail](#)


## 39. Motor Driver Module (e.g., L298N or L293D)
Acts as a high-current amplifier that allows low-power Arduino pins to control the speed and direction of high-power DC motors or stepper motors.
[Explore Motor Driver Module in detail](#)


## 40. Gas Sensor (MQ Series, e.g., MQ-2)
Detects the presence and concentration of various gases in the air, such as LPG, smoke, propane, methane, and carbon monoxide.
[Explore Gas Sensor in detail](#)


## 41. OLED Display (e.g., SSD1306)
A crisp, power-efficient miniature screen used to display complex graphics, custom fonts, and data readouts without requiring a backlight.
[Explore OLED Display in detail](#)


## 42. Sound Sensor Module (e.g., KY-038)
Detects ambient noise levels or specific sound spikes. This is a great choice if you're building a voice-controlled obstacle-avoiding robot.
[Explore Sound Sensor Module in detail](#)


## 43. Joystick Module (e.g., KY-023)
Provides two-axis analog input (X and Y) plus a central digital push-button. This is a highly interactive and heavily requested input device for beginners to play with.
[Explore Joystick Module in detail](#)


## 44. RGB LED
Contains red, green, and blue light-emitting diodes in a single package. By adjusting the voltage sent to each pin, you can mix them to emit almost any color of light.
[Explore RGB LED in detail](#)


## 45. Micro SD Card Module
Allows the Arduino to read and write data to a standard microSD card using SPI communication. Essential for data logging, storing large arrays, or keeping project data saved offline.
[Explore Micro SD Card Module in detail](#)
